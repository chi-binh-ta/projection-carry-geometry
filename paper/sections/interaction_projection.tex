\section{Interaction Matrices and Diagonal Projection}

Given digit vectors $a$ and $b$, define the interaction matrix
\[
  M_{ij} = a_i b_j.
\]
Each entry records one pairwise digit interaction.

The raw coefficient at degree $k$ is the anti-diagonal sum
\[
  c_k = \sum_{i+j=k} M_{ij}.
\]
Equivalently, diagonal projection is the pushforward of mass under the map
$(i,j)\mapsto i+j$. At the support level this becomes the additive sumset
\[
  \operatorname{supp}(a) + \operatorname{supp}(b)
  = \{i+j : i \in \operatorname{supp}(a), j \in \operatorname{supp}(b)\}.
\]
